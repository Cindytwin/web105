Anne Frank: The Diary of a Young Girl

Summary

"Auto Biography:" Anne Frank "Written By" Anne Frank "This autobiography is about how a young girl transforms into a young teenager.
She goes through mental changes and vents her discomforts and cares about the world
and her unfortunate circumstances. Although she is young and innocent she knows a lot
about life. It is absolutely incredible how she can perfectly convey her feelings straight
onto paper, it is so touching."

"Anne Frank goes through so much during her time in the secret annex. She talks about her crushes
and what food she eats. What things she looks forward to, and things she's afraid of. She writes about
her family and how she personally gets along with each one of them. Through her roller coaster ride of
feelings she makes the best out of the worlds biggest nightmare. "

"I have read this book in middle school a while back. I had repurchased this book because I didn't quite
remember the story anymore. This was a book I fully enjoyed, despite the tragedy of her story, I feel I
appreciated it more the second time around more than the first."

"This is a quote by Anne Frank:" "I must uphold my ideals for perhaps the time will come when I shall be able to carry them out."
