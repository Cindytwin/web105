Cherry Blossoms

The Cherry Blossom Tree in Tokyo are very popular in Japan culture.
In Japanese they call this tree "Sakura". They incorporate this flower into drinks food and much more.
There is also a big festival in the spring time held for the blooming of these gorgeous trees.
If you didn't know these trees blanket most if Japan but they also come in many varirties.
These flowers die rather quickly, so if you ever plan on visiting the day of the festival is held; be sure to arrive promptly.
I think by far the most interesting fact about this marvelous tree is that the oldest Sakura tree is over 2,000 years old. It's circumference is
13.5 meters. It's name is "Jidai Zakura". You can find it in the Jissou Temple in the Yamanashi Prefecture.

Sky Tree

Sky Tree if you didn't know is an observation and broadcasting tower. Tokyo Skytree is located in Sumida, Tokyo.
In 2011 it became the tallest tower in the world at 2,080 feet. Now it remains the second tallest after Burj Khalifa.
The structure is similar to that of a tripod, it's cylindrical top was designed to offer views to the river and to it's beautiful
city scape. This fantastic building also features a glass floor located at the upper observatory level where tourists can peer down
at Tokyo below their feet. Because Tokyo, and most of Japan is prone to earthquakes, it was built to withstand such interferences.
In the center of the building a concrete shaft was built to renforce the outer structure. This shaft is also attached ot to the fame work
and other parts that cushion the building when it shakes.

Tokyo offers many cuisines from all over, but what they're popular for is Ramen and Sushi.
If you enjoy a good bowl of Ramen you might need to wait in line for a good resturant or have a seat

For the best sushi in Tokyo you should visit "Sushi Saito" it's pricey but definetly worth the price for a flavourful bite.
For the best ramen in Tokyo you should visit "Fuunji" this is a more affordable place in serves a variety of ramens.
