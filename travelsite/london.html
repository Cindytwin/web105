Big Ben

Iconic to this cities landscape Big Ben is the nickname to that of Great Bell.
In 2012 it was renamed to Elizabeth Tower to mark the Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II, the "current queen" of
the United Kingdom. This tower was designed in the Neo-Gothic era by Augustus Pugin. This fine building stands at
315 feet. An interesting fact is that the dials measure to 23 feet in diameter. Another fact would be that is uses it's
original victorian mechanics but a motor is used as back-up if the original mechanism fails that day.

London Eye

Although I am deathly afraid of heights I could not pass up a chance to see The London Eye.
This structure is also popular for it's nickname but it's original name is "Millennium Wheel".
This by far the most popluar tourist attraction of London. It welcomes about 3 million visitors a year!
It reaches a height of 443 feet tall and 394 feet in diameter! It is the world largest cantilevered observation wheel.
